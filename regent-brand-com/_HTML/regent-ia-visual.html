Key Observations

Notes for Wilson Redesign

1
Flat architecture. The site is only 2 levels deep for most paths. Brand content pages (Spa, Dining, M&E, About) are all single long-scroll pages with zero sub-navigation. This is great for Wilson — straightforward component mapping.
2
All hotel locations are external. Every hotel listed on the Hotels & Resorts page drives users to its own separate hotel site (hoteldetail pages or standalone domains like santamonica.regenthotels.com). The Brand.com site itself is purely a marketing/wayfinding layer — it does not host hotel content directly.
3
Hotels & Resorts is the hub page. It's the most content-dense page — 10 open properties each with hero image + copy + CTA, plus a 5-tab "Coming Soon" carousel. All CTAs route externally to hotel sites. This page will need careful component planning.
4
External everywhere. All 10 hotel locations, all 3 Residences, and the homepage hotel carousel all link out to external sites. The Brand.com redesign scope is the marketing shell only — the brand story pages, hub listing, and global navigation. Hotel-level pages live on their own sites with their own nav (Home, Rooms & Suites, Dining, M&E, Weddings, Offers).
5
Only 2 nested subpages exist. Taste Studio (/deals/global-offers/taste-studio) under Offers, and Cannes Film Festival (/about/cannes-film-festival) under About. Everything else is a flat single page.
6
Persistent "Book Now" bar. Every page has a sticky bottom CTA bar — "BOOK NOW" on brand pages, "VIEW PRICES" on hotel detail pages. This is a global pattern that Wilson should account for as a persistent component.
